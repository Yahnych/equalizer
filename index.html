Equalizer

An audio analysis tool for real-time and choreographed visualizations.

Source Code · MIT

Start by dragging an audio file — preferably a wav, mp3, or ogg — onto the gray box. Once loaded a rotated staff will be displayed. Hit the spacebar to play and pause the music. Once audio starts playing you'll notice some visual activity, 16 bands representing different amplitudes at equally spaced frequency bandwidths.

You can hit the gray circle to record “beats” from the equalizer to the staff. Once recording has been enabled you can click and drag to scrub through the staff and scroll (i.e: use your computer's mousewheel) to change the scale of the staff's viewport.

Be careful: recording overdubs so you can build on top of what already exists on the staff.
